About The Internet


  • 1975 – Bill Gate’s career started where he dropped out of Harvard to start his company with his friend Paul Allen.
  • 1993 – About 90% of PCS were running Microsoft windows. Microsoft became extremely wealthy, making the employee stock option millionaires.
  • Gates brought companies like lotus, novel, and even IDM to their knees. At one point Gates had as much power as the president.
  • Summer 1994 – Netscape communications was launched with Jim Clark and his team. It was at the time the fastest growing web. With Clark watching over the employees were spreading the news about the web.
  • 13th of October 1994 – Navigator hit the streets. It was a runaway success. They had a million downloads very quick.
  • 1995 - Internet was becoming the full scale phenom, and Netscape was the head of it. It was looking particularly good for Netscape at this point.
  • In 1997, 72% of the business was also owned by Netscape. But Internet Explorer 4.0 was released in October 1997 and browser war waves were modified. It pursued the released specifications of W3C faster and more loyally than Netscape Navigator 4.0.
  • One was a matter of resources: Netscape began with almost 90% market share and a great deal of public goodwill, but it became financially fragile as a relatively small business which produced most of its profits from what was essentially one commodity.
  • Gross sales of Netscape have never matched the cash-on-the-hand earnings of Microsoft.
  • The other, most significant, advantage was that Microsoft Windows has an industry lock that could be used to take over IE. IE arrived with every Windows clone and thus Microsoft somehow managed to grow market share even though IE's prior versions were marginally less than Netscape's browser.
  • And IE remained free because Windows' huge sales helped to fund its development and promotion, leading to drastic updates to Netscape so close it could not be downloaded and installed.
